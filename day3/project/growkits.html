Grifola frondosa, also known as hen-of-the-woods, or maitake, is a polypore mushroom that grows at the base of trees, particularly old growth oaks or maples. It is known for its quick fruiting stage, where it decays into a dark, inky substance. The flesh is best eaten as soon as it is harvested.
